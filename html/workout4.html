Dumbbell Swing Through

10 Reps x 4 Sets

The dumbbell swing through is a dynamic exercise that involves swinging a dumbbell between your legs and then up to shoulder height in a controlled manner, engaging your hips and core muscles. It combines elements of a hip hinge and a shoulder raise, providing a full-body workout.

One Legged Toe Raise

10 Reps x 4 Sets

" A one-legged toe raise is a simple exercise where you lift your body onto the ball of one foot, raising your heel as high as possible while keeping the rest of your body stable. It targets the calf muscles and helps improve ankle strength and stability.

Reverse Lunge

10 Reps x 4 Sets

A reverse lunge is a lower body exercise where you step backward with one leg, lowering your body until both knees are bent at a 90-degree angle. Then, you push off the back foot to return to the starting position. It primarily works the muscles in the thighs, hips, and buttocks, providing benefits for leg strength and stability.

Seated One Legged Toe Raise

10 Reps x 4 Sets

A seated one-legged toe raise is an exercise performed while sitting, typically on the edge of a chair or bench. With one foot planted firmly on the ground, you lift the other leg and raise the heel as high as possible while keeping the toes on the ground. This exercise targets the muscles in the calf of the raised leg and helps improve ankle strength and mobility. It's a seated variation of the traditional one-legged toe raise.

Side Lunge

10 Reps x 4 Sets

A side lunge is a lower body exercise where you step to the side, keeping one leg straight while bending the other knee, and then push back to the starting position. It engages muscles in the thighs, hips, and buttocks, promoting strength and flexibility in the lower body. Side lunges are effective for targeting different muscle groups compared to forward or reverse lunges and can contribute to overall lower body conditioning.

Squat

10 Reps x 4 Sets

A squat is a fundamental lower body exercise where you lower your hips by bending your knees and hips, as if you're sitting back into an imaginary chair. The goal is to lower your body until your thighs are parallel to the ground or as far as your flexibility allows. Squats engage multiple muscle groups, including the quadriceps, hamstrings, glutes, and core. They are a versatile and effective exercise for building lower body strength, endurance, and overall functional fitness.

Stationary Lunge

10 Reps x 4 Sets

A stationary lunge is a lower body exercise that involves taking a step forward or backward into a split stance and then lowering the body by bending both knees. Unlike the forward or reverse lunge where you step continuously, in a stationary lunge, the feet remain in place throughout the exercise. This movement primarily targets the muscles in the thighs, hips, and buttocks, providing benefits for leg strength, stability, and balance. It's a static variation of the lunge exercise.

Stiff Legged Dead Lift

10 Reps x 4 Sets

A stiff-legged deadlift, also known as a straight-legged deadlift, is a strength training exercise that targets the muscles of the posterior chain, including the hamstrings and lower back. In this exercise, you typically start in a standing position with your feet hip-width apart, knees slightly bent. Holding a barbell or dumbbells in front of you, you hinge at your hips, lowering the weights towards the floor while keeping your back straight. The emphasis is on hip flexion and maintaining a slight bend in the knees, with minimal bending of the spine. Stiff-legged deadlifts are effective for developing hamstring flexibility, strength, and overall posterior chain engagement.
